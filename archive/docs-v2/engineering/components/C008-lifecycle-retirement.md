# C008 - Lifecycle & Retirement

Removes or relocates a concept already in the corpus — `delete` removes it outright, `deprecate` marks it in place with a reason and optional replacement pointer, `move` relocates it to a new path — repairing every inbound link the action would otherwise orphan by the single **repair-to-successor** rule, and surfaces retirement candidates from measurable signals (staleness, source drift, supersession) for the steward's judgment.

C008 is the pipeline's **mutation-of-existing-concepts** component, the counterpart to C003's authoring of concept content: where C003 `create`s and `revise`s concept content, C008 turns the steward's removal, deprecation, or relocation judgment into a change to a concept already in the corpus. It fulfills deprecation over deletion (O003-R001) and retirement review (O003-R002) outright, and carries assisted upkeep (O004-R003) on both sides of the loop — surfacing retirement candidates for the steward like the other detect faces, and carrying out the confirmed removal, deprecation, or relocation by verb so nothing is hand-edited. `deprecate` and `delete` are held apart as **two verbs, not one with a mode** — `deprecate` marks in place and never removes a file; `delete` removes the file outright. Neither is a default: the steward picks per item, since only they know whether the material is worth keeping as a marked-in-place pointer or is simply obsolete. Deprecation and supersession are themselves **one mechanism**: a concept is deprecated with a reason, and carries a replacement pointer only when one exists — "superseded" is simply the case where that pointer is set. Like C002, C008 both surfaces candidates for judgment and executes the steward's confirmed action.

**C008 is the one component besides C003 that writes into a concept it isn't itself acting on.** Deleting, moving, or merging-away a concept can orphan the links inside every concept that referenced it; C008 repairs those mechanically by the repair-to-successor rule — through C004's link-syntax helper, never composing new prose. This is a deliberate, narrow exception to C003 being the sole *author* of concept content, not a second authoring path. See [ADR009](../drs/ADR009-retirement-relocation-and-link-repair-model.md) for the original deletion/relocation reasoning and [ADR012](../drs/ADR012-repair-to-successor-link-model.md) for the unified repair rule that superseded its per-operation split.

## Data model

- **Deprecation marker.** A Wiki convention layered on OKF's tolerant extension-key and heading conventions, read and written through C004:
  - **`deprecated: true`** — a frontmatter boolean, the cheap discovery key (mirrors C010's `type: charter` as a semantic convention C004 neither registers nor constrains).
  - **`# Deprecation`** — a body section, required once `deprecated` is set: prose stating the reason, and, when a replacement exists, a reasoned cross-link to it (a bundle-relative link with the reason in the surrounding prose, per OKF §5.3 and C003's link convention) — never a raw frontmatter path, so the replacement pointer is a knowledge cross-link like any other.
- **Repaired-link result** (transient, not written to the corpus beyond the edit itself). One per referring concept `delete`, `move`, or a `merge`'s `delete` step mechanically edits: `{ concept-id, before, after }` — the link text before and after the edit, so the steward can see exactly what changed. The `after` reflects the repair-to-successor rule: retargeted to the new path (`move`), redirected to the replacement/merge target (`delete` with a replacement), or reduced to plain text (`delete` with none).
- **Review candidate** (transient). One per referring concept a `delete` surfaces after repairing its link: `{ concept-id, deleted-concept-id, rationale }` — a prompt to check whether that concept's own content still holds up without what it cited, distinct from the mechanical repair itself. Only a `delete` with `content_preserved=false` produces review candidates: `move` leaves content untouched, and a `merge`'s `delete` step passes `content_preserved=true` because its `revise` step already folded the content into the merge target, so neither raises the "does the referrer still make sense" question. The discriminator is the `content_preserved` flag, not a `delete`-vs-`merge` distinction inside the verb — a supersession `delete` (a different concept as replacement) leaves the flag false and does surface candidates ([ADR014](../drs/ADR014-merge-composition-integrity.md)).
- **Retirement candidate** (transient, not written to the corpus). One per concept the periodic sweep flags: `{ concept-id, signals: [{ kind ∈ { staleness, drift, supersession }, rationale }], candidate-replacement? }`. A concept can carry more than one signal at once; they are not deduplicated into a single verdict.

## Interfaces

C008 exposes an in-process face. Every corpus read and write goes through C004. Each mutation performs its link-repair and its recency/history recording (C007) and reindex (C005) as **internal effects of the verb**, not calls a caller must make afterward ([ADR011](../drs/ADR011-concept-verb-surface.md)).

- **`deprecate(concept_id, reason, [replacement_concept_id]) → concept`** — writes the marker via C004. Refuses a `replacement_concept_id` that does not resolve to an existing concept, and refuses the charter concept outright. Never deletes anything and never edits another concept — its file simply stays put, so no inbound link needs repair. Records `Deprecation` (fresh) or `Update` (edit) and reindexes as terminal effects.
- **`delete(concept_id, [replacement_concept_id], content_preserved=false) → { removed_path, links_repaired[], review_candidates[] }`** — deletes the concept's file via C004. Reads `C005.inbound_links(concept_id)` first to find every referrer; for each, repairs the reference by the repair-to-successor rule — **redirected** to `replacement_concept_id` when one is given, else **stripped** to plain text — through C004's link-syntax helper. Refuses a `replacement_concept_id` that does not resolve; refuses the charter concept. Records `Retirement` for the deleted concept and `Update` for each repaired referrer (C007), and reindexes, as terminal effects. Returns the repaired links and, separately, the referrers as `review_candidates` — **unless `content_preserved` is set**, which suppresses them. `content_preserved` asserts that the departing concept's content survives at `replacement_concept_id`: a bare steward `delete` leaves it `false` (a supersession replacement is a different concept that may not carry what each referrer relied on, so review is warranted); the `delete` step of a `merge` passes `true` (its `revise` step folded the content into the target the links now point at). When invoked as that composition step, `delete` defers its commit to the enclosing `merge` transaction rather than committing on its own ([ADR014](../drs/ADR014-merge-composition-integrity.md)). (This is the former `retire`, extended with the optional replacement and the `content_preserved` flag.)
- **`move(concept_id, new_path) → concept`** — moves the concept's file to `new_path` via C004's path helpers. Reads `C005.inbound_links(concept_id)` and retargets each referrer's link to `new_path` (through C004's link-syntax helper). Content is unchanged; only its path and the links pointing to it are. Records `Update` for the moved concept and each retargeted referrer (C007) and reindexes. Produces no review candidates. (This is the former `relocate`.)
- **`deprecation_of(concept_id) → { reason, replacement-concept-id? } | none`** — reads the marker, `none` when the concept carries no `deprecated` flag.
- **`retirement_candidates(scope?) → candidate[]`** — the periodic sweep behind the product's **Survey** operation: reads staleness from `C007.recency`/`history`, drift from `C006.revalidate_all`, and runs its own agent scan through C004 for supersession — self-contained, no dependency on C002 or C005 for candidate discovery, the same posture [ADR004](../drs/ADR004-triage-disposition-model.md) set for C002. Excludes concepts already deprecated and excludes the charter. Read-only.

## Behavior

- **Deprecate in place; delete removes; move relocates; steward picks, no default.** `deprecate` never removes a file; `delete` always does; `move` changes only location. `retirement_candidates` recommends nothing about which to use — it surfaces signals, and the steward chooses `deprecate`, `delete`, `move`, or nothing.
- **One repair rule: repair to the successor.** When a concept leaves or moves, every inbound link is repaired to its successor — the new path (`move`), the replacement/merge target (`delete` with a replacement, and the `delete` step of `merge`), or plain text when there is no successor (`delete` with none). `deprecate` needs no repair, since its file stays and the links still resolve. This single rule replaces the earlier retarget-vs-strip split ([ADR012](../drs/ADR012-repair-to-successor-link-model.md)).
- **Mechanical repair only, never authored prose.** The one edit a mutation makes to a concept other than the one named is rewriting a link's syntax — through C004's link-syntax helper, which recognizes and rewrites a bundle-relative link without touching surrounding text. C008 never rewrites a sentence, drops a claim, or composes new prose in a concept it doesn't own.
- **Repair, then surface for review — but only where content actually left.** `delete` fixes the syntax (no dangling link survives) and separately returns `review_candidates`, because a referrer's prose may have depended on the deleted concept's content in a way no mechanical edit — not even a redirect to a replacement — can judge. It suppresses them only when the caller passes `content_preserved=true`, asserting the depended-on content survives at the replacement. `move` and `merge` raise no such question: `move` changes only location, and `merge`'s `delete` step passes `content_preserved=true` because its `revise` step folded the content into the merge target its links now point at.
- **A replacement here means supersession, not merge.** A `delete` or `deprecate` carrying a replacement *supersedes*: it redirects (or leaves resolving) inbound links to the replacement but does **not** fold the removed concept's content into it — the steward chooses it when the replacement already covers the ground. Reconciling the removed concept's content into the survivor first is `merge` (C003). The steward's test is whether the survivor's content should change: yes → `merge`; no → `delete`/`deprecate` with a replacement. This is exactly why a bare `delete` (with `content_preserved` false) surfaces review candidates and a `merge` (whose `delete` step passes it true) does not.
- **Recommend, never delete, deprecate, or move automatically.** `retirement_candidates` only surfaces; only a direct `deprecate`, `delete`, or `move` call changes anything, mirroring C002's recommend-and-confirm posture and the architecture's never-automatic principle applied to removal, not just admission.
- **Materialization is a verb effect.** Each mutation records recency and history (C007) and reindexes (C005) as its own terminal effect, so no caller orchestrates `record` or `reindex` ([ADR011](../drs/ADR011-concept-verb-surface.md)).
- **Self-contained supersession scan.** The scan for "a newer concept covers this ground" is C008's own agent pass over the corpus through C004, not a call into C002's overlap machinery or C005's graph — consistent with the self-contained precedent ADR004 and ADR005 established.

## Edge cases

- **Deprecating with no replacement**: `reason` is required, `replacement_concept_id` is not; the `# Deprecation` section omits the replacement link entirely.
- **Deprecating an already-deprecated concept**: edits the reason and/or replacement in place; recorded as `Update`, not a second `Deprecation` event.
- **`replacement_concept_id` does not resolve** (on `deprecate` or `delete`): refused outright — unlike an ordinary reasoned cross-link (which tolerates a forward reference per OKF §5.3), a replacement pointer, and a redirect target, must name something real.
- **Deleting with a replacement**: every inbound link is redirected to the replacement rather than stripped; `review_candidates` are still surfaced, because the replacement is a different concept that may not carry what each referrer relied on.
- **Deleting as the `delete` step of a `merge`**: called with `content_preserved=true`, so inbound links are redirected to the merge target and `review_candidates` are suppressed — the preceding `revise` folded the content into that target, so no referrer's claim is left unsupported. This step also defers its commit to the enclosing `merge` transaction, so a failure here leaves the whole `merge` uncommitted rather than removing `from` after its content was folded in ([ADR014](../drs/ADR014-merge-composition-integrity.md)).
- **Deleting a concept with no inbound links**: `links_repaired` and `review_candidates` are both empty; the deletion proceeds regardless.
- **Deleting a concept linked from more than one place in the same referrer**: each occurrence is repaired independently; the referrer appears once in `review_candidates` regardless of how many links it held.
- **Moving a concept to a path that already holds a concept**: refused — `move` never overwrites an existing concept, mirroring C003's `create` slug-collision handling.
- **The charter concept**: `deprecate` and `delete` both refuse it, and `retirement_candidates` never includes it — revising its purpose is `C010.revise_charter`, not a retirement. (`move` is not restricted, but the charter's conventional location is stable.)
- **A concept flagged by more than one signal** (e.g. stale and drifted): surfaced once, carrying both signal entries.
- **Retirement candidate already deprecated**: excluded from `retirement_candidates` output.

## Relationships

- **C004 - OKF Conformance**: sole path to corpus content — the deprecation-marker frontmatter/body convention, the file-delete and path-move helpers, and the link-syntax helper used to strip, retarget, or redirect a referrer's link. C008 encodes no OKF structural literal.
- **C005 - Index & Navigation**: `inbound_links` is load-bearing — `delete` and `move` both read it to find every concept they must mechanically repair. Each mutation triggers a reindex as an internal effect. C005 applies no exclusion to deprecated concepts (they stay indexed, like the charter); a deleted concept simply no longer appears once its file is gone.
- **C006 - Source Revalidation**: `retirement_candidates` reads `revalidate_all` and treats a `drifted` verdict as one retirement signal (O003-R002's "source drift"). C008 performs no fetch itself.
- **C007 - Currency Tracking**: `deprecate` records `Deprecation` (fresh) or `Update` (edit); `delete` records `Retirement` against the deleted concept plus `Update` against every repaired referrer; `move` records `Update` against both the moved concept and every retargeted referrer. `retirement_candidates` also reads `recency`/`history` as its staleness signal. These recordings are internal effects of the verbs.
- **C010 - Charter**: excludes the charter from `retirement_candidates` and refuses both `deprecate` and `delete` on it. When `C010.reconcile()` surfaces an out-of-scope concept, the steward may direct C008 to `deprecate` or `delete` it — C010 detects scope drift, C008 executes.
- **C003 - Integration Authoring**: `merge` is the one place the two compose — C003's `revise` folds `from` into `into`, then `C008.delete(from, replacement=into, content_preserved=true)` removes `from` and redirects its inbound links to `into` (review candidates suppressed, per above). The two steps run in one transaction that `merge` commits atomically ([ADR014](../drs/ADR014-merge-composition-integrity.md)), so C008's `delete` defers its commit when composed. Otherwise no runtime dependency: C008's mechanical link edits never touch a concept's authored prose, only a link's syntax, so C003's ownership of *authoring* content is undiminished — only its claim to being the sole *writer* of any kind is narrowed (see [ADR009](../drs/ADR009-retirement-relocation-and-link-repair-model.md)).
- **C002 - Triage**: no relationship — C002's duplication check runs against incoming material; C008's supersession scan runs against the existing corpus, a distinct pass over different material.
- **Boundary**: C008 owns marking a concept deprecated, deleting or moving a concept, mechanically repairing the links those actions orphan or retarget by the repair-to-successor rule, and surfacing retirement candidates from staleness, drift, and supersession signals. It never authors new prose in a concept it isn't acting on, never judges whether a referrer's content still holds up (only surfaces the question), and never judges scope or duplication at intake (C002, C010).

## Success criteria

- **Steward's choice preserved**: no path picks `deprecate`, `delete`, or `move` on the steward's behalf — `retirement_candidates` never mutates the corpus, and each mutation only runs when called directly.
- **No dangling link survives a deletion or move**: after `delete` or `move` completes, `C005.dangling_links` contains no link that used to target the affected concept — verifiable by deleting or moving a linked-to concept and re-running `dangling_links`.
- **Repair follows the successor**: after `delete` with a replacement (or a `merge`), the repaired links point at the replacement/target; after `delete` with none, they are plain text; after `move`, they point at the new path — verifiable by inspecting `links_repaired`.
- **Mechanical repair only**: a diff of any referrer C008 edits shows only the link's markup changed (text or target) — no other line differs.
- **Review surfaced where content left, suppressed where it didn't**: every referrer a `delete` with `content_preserved=false` repairs also appears in `review_candidates`; the same `delete` with `content_preserved=true` (as `merge` calls it) and a `move` produce none — verifiable by deleting a linked-to concept with the flag false (both lists name the referrers), again with it true (only `links_repaired` does), and by moving one (neither does).
- **Replacement resolves**: a `deprecate` or `delete` call carrying a replacement only succeeds when it resolves to an existing concept.
- **Charter excluded**: `retirement_candidates` never includes the charter concept; `deprecate` and `delete` both refuse it.
- **One-owner invariant preserved**: C008 contains no OKF structural literal — ADR001's grep-able check stays clean with C008 present.

## Notes

- The deprecation marker (`deprecated: true` plus a `# Deprecation` body section) is a Wiki convention recorded here rather than a separate ADR, the same treatment C007 gave its log-kind vocabulary — promote it if it becomes contested.
- The original retirement/relocation/link-repair model — steward's free choice of mechanism and mechanical-repair-plus-review-candidates for deletion — is captured in [ADR009](../drs/ADR009-retirement-relocation-and-link-repair-model.md); the unified repair-to-successor rule, `delete`'s optional replacement, and the review-candidate narrowing that support `merge` are captured in [ADR012](../drs/ADR012-repair-to-successor-link-model.md). The `retire`→`delete` and `relocate`→`move` renames follow the verb surface in [ADR011](../drs/ADR011-concept-verb-surface.md).
- Retirement review is surfaced through the product's **Survey** operation, which C011 - Curation Operations owns: C011 reads `retirement_candidates` as one Survey face alongside C006's `revalidate_all` and C010's `reconcile`, de-duplicates the drift and staleness facts a candidate already folds in, and pairs a candidate against a C009 `thin` finding on the same concept as an explicit expand-vs-retire choice (see [ADR016](../drs/ADR016-survey-lint-aggregation-ownership.md)).
- The `content_preserved` parameter on `delete` and the one-commit-or-none atomicity of the `merge` composition are captured with alternatives in [ADR014](../drs/ADR014-merge-composition-integrity.md) and [CR005](../../crs/CR005-merge-composition-integrity.md); they make review-candidate suppression a truthful argument rather than a merge-only branch, and close the partial-`merge` duplicate window.
