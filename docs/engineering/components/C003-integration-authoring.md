# C003 - Integration Authoring

Turns a triaged item, another concept, or the steward's direction into concept content — composing it, attaching its source citation, and cross-linking related concepts with the reason for each stated — through two write verbs, `create` (a new concept) and `revise` (an existing one in place), and the `merge` composition built from them.

C003 is the curation pipeline's **write stage** and the only component that *authors* concept content — composing prose, citations, and reasoned links. Its verbs realize the concept surface's write half ([ADR011](../drs/ADR011-concept-verb-surface.md)): `create` mints a new concept from an admitted task; `revise` folds a source into an existing concept in place; `merge` combines two existing concepts as a composition of `revise` + C008's `delete`. C008 - Lifecycle & Retirement additionally performs narrow, mechanical link-repair edits to concepts it isn't itself acting on (stripping, retargeting, or redirecting a link's syntax when it deletes, moves, or merges that link's target), so C003 is not the *only* writer of any kind, just the only author (see [ADR009](../drs/ADR009-retirement-relocation-and-link-repair-model.md), [ADR012](../drs/ADR012-repair-to-successor-link-model.md)). It fulfills cited claims (O001-R001) and intent-driven authoring (O004-R001), and shares reasoned cross-links (O005-R002) with C005 - Index & Navigation: C003 authors the link with its reason, C005 makes the graph navigable and keeps it intact.

Every write verb is **atomic including its effects**: composing content, promoting provenance, recomputing citations, recording recency and history (C007), and regenerating the affected index (C005) all happen as part of the verb, not as separate calls a caller must remember to make afterward ([ADR011](../drs/ADR011-concept-verb-surface.md)). One capability is deferred: authoring a *new* concept from the steward's own knowledge with no task (source-less `create`) awaits a future PDR, so `create` stays admitted-task-gated; revising an existing, already-provenanced concept from steward direction is in scope.

## Data model

C003 owns **no staging artifact of its own**. Its output is the corpus itself — concepts and the provenance assets they cite — whose format is C004's, not C003's. C003 owns the *composition*, and it settles one path convention ADR002 deferred to it.

- **Authored concept.** An OKF concept C003 composes and writes via C004: frontmatter (a non-empty `type`, a `title`, and any steward-supplied metadata; the `timestamp` value is C007's, set as a verb effect), a structural markdown body, a `# Citations` block, and cross-links. C003 owns which fields and content go in; C004 owns the surrounding format.
- **Provenance area** (`/references/`, bundle root). The corpus-side location a durable source is promoted into at authoring — settling the promotion boundary C001 and [ADR002](../drs/ADR002-intake-staging-and-durable-source.md) left open. A promoted source is a **git-tracked asset, not a concept**: UTF-8 text for textual sources, or the bounded binary-asset fallback for non-text sources. C004's plain-text-concept `error` rule excludes declared snapshot assets, so a promoted binary source never reads as a conformance failure.
- **Citation.** A numbered `# Citations` block (OKF §8), rendered through C004's citation helper. **Concept-level by default**: a single-source concept's block covers all its claims. **Inline per-claim** only when a concept draws on more than one source (after a `revise` that folds in a second source, or a `merge`): individual claims carry numbered markers so a reader can tell which source backs which claim. **Link target depends on how the source was durably held** ([ADR007](../drs/ADR007-citation-link-form-for-drift-revalidation.md)): an in-place source cites its existing repo path, unchanged; a captured-snapshot source cites the live `origin` URL as the primary link (OKF §8 permits an absolute-URL citation), naming the archived `/references/` asset as a durable fallback copy in the surrounding prose. This keeps the citation pointed at the actual external material and gives C006 - Source Revalidation a live locator to read directly off the concept. **The block always mirrors the body it accompanies**: on a `revise`, C003 recomputes it from the reconciled body's inline markers — a citation no claim still references is dropped, and the rest renumbered contiguously ([ADR010](../drs/ADR010-citation-pruning-on-merge.md)) — the same way a fresh `create` derives its citation block from what's actually written rather than carrying anything forward unreferenced.
- **Cross-link.** A bundle-relative link (`/…`, C004's link helper) whose **reason is stated in the surrounding prose**, because OKF §5.3 conveys the relationship kind in prose, not in the link itself. C003 emits no link without its reason.

## Interfaces

C003 exposes an in-process face. Every read of a task goes through C001, every OKF read/write goes through C004, and the only corpus writes are concept content and its promoted provenance asset.

- **`create(task_id) → concept_id`** — mints a **new** concept. Refuses unless the task's recorded `decision` is `admit`. Reads the task and its `durable_ref` (C001); promotes the durable source into `/references/`; composes the concept and its `# Citations` via C004; writes confirmed cross-links; writes the concept file at a C004-derived slug/path; and, as terminal effects, records `Creation` (C007) and reindexes (C005). Returns the concept's C004-derived id. (This is the former `author` with `direction.mode = keep-new`.)
- **`revise(concept_id, source) → concept_id`** — folds `source` into an **existing** concept **in place**, preserving its id, history, and inbound links, and recomputing citations to match the reconciled body. `source` is one of: an **admitted task** (the former `author` with `direction.mode = merge` — unions cross-links, reconciles prose per the steward's note, promotes and cites the task's source); **another concept** (the reconciliation step `merge` builds on); or the **steward's direction** (a correction or refinement to an existing, already-provenanced concept — O004-R003, the interface C006 and C010 reference for correcting content). Records `Update` (C007) and reindexes (C005) as terminal effects.
- **`merge(from_concept_id, into_concept_id, [note]) → concept_id`** — combines two **existing** concepts, defined as `revise(into_concept_id, source=from_concept_id)` followed by `C008.delete(from_concept_id, replacement=into_concept_id)`. The surviving `into` concept keeps its id and history; `from`'s inbound links are redirected to `into` by C008's repair-to-successor rule ([ADR012](../drs/ADR012-repair-to-successor-link-model.md)), and `merge` surfaces no review candidates because its own `revise` step preserves the content at `into`. Returns the surviving `into_concept_id`. Fulfills the in-corpus case of the duplication-remediation choice O003-R003 already names.
- **`promote_source(task) → citation_ref`** *(internal effect of `create`/`revise`-from-task)* — promotes the durable source from C001's staging into `/references/` and returns the citable bundle-relative path; for an already-version-controlled in-place source, returns the existing repo path and copies no bytes. Not a steward-facing operation.
- **`propose_links(concept | task_id) → link_candidate[]`** *(internal, advisory, read-only)* — the cross-link candidate scan: reuses C002's overlap candidates when present, else runs its own agent scan reading corpus concepts through C004, returning each candidate with a suggested reason for the steward to confirm, drop, or edit. It writes nothing; `create`/`revise` only write links the steward confirmed. Self-contained: no C005 dependency ([ADR004](../drs/ADR004-triage-disposition-model.md) precedent).

## Behavior

- **`create`/`revise`-from-task author only what the steward admitted.** No task-sourced path writes a concept for a task whose recorded `decision` is not `admit`. Integration stays never-automatic: C002 decides *whether and what*, C003 does *how*, one admitted item at a time. A steward-direction `revise` acts on an existing concept the steward names directly — its adjudication is the steward's own direction, not a triage gate.
- **Intent in, authoring out.** From the admitted task, another concept, or the steward's direction and note, C003 performs all composition, citation, linking, and metadata. The steward never hand-edits the corpus (O004-R001, O004-R003).
- **Cite what it writes — and only what it still writes.** Every authored concept carries a `# Citations` entry resolving to its originating source (O001-R001) — a promoted `/references/` asset, or the in-place repo path for an already-versioned source. A single source covers the whole concept; a concept fused from more than one source adds inline per-claim markers so provenance stays legible. When a `revise` *corrects* a claim rather than adding beside it, the superseded citation is dropped along with the claim it backed, not carried forward unreferenced ([ADR010](../drs/ADR010-citation-pruning-on-merge.md)) — the correction and its prior citation remain fully recoverable via git history and C007's `history`, just no longer live in the document. A steward-direction `revise` that only refines wording or structure changes no citation; it must not introduce a net-new claim without a source (that comes through a task).
- **Promote at authoring, cite by locator.** The durable source C001 held in staging is promoted into `/references/` as a git-tracked asset regardless of citation form — the archived copy always exists as the drift baseline C006 later hashes on demand. The citation link itself follows the source's kind ([ADR007](../drs/ADR007-citation-link-form-for-drift-revalidation.md)): an in-place source already version-controlled alongside the corpus is cited at its existing path with no copy; a captured-snapshot source is cited by its live `origin` URL, with the archived `/references/` path named alongside it in prose. Because nothing outside the task pointed at the staging path, the one-time relocation of the archived copy is safe (ADR002).
- **Reasoned cross-links.** Links are authored with their reason in prose (OKF §5.3). Candidates are proposed by `propose_links`; the steward confirms; the write verb writes only confirmed links. A link target that is not yet written is allowed — OKF §5.3 requires consumers to tolerate not-yet-written knowledge, and inbound-link integrity is C005's, not a block on authoring.
- **`revise` preserves identity.** A `revise` folds its source into the existing concept **in place**: it keeps the concept-id and history and recomputes citations to match the reconciled body rather than blindly unioning the old set with the new — a claim that survives reconciliation keeps its citation; a claim the revision corrects away takes its citation with it ([ADR010](../drs/ADR010-citation-pruning-on-merge.md)). No new id is minted, so inbound links stay valid — referential integrity holds by construction. `create` instead mints a fresh concept.
- **`merge` composes, never re-implements.** `merge` is exactly `revise` (fold `from` into `into`) plus `C008.delete(from, replacement=into)`; it introduces no third authoring or removal mechanism. The redirect of `from`'s inbound links and the `Retirement` log entry for `from` are C008's, via ADR012's repair-to-successor rule.
- **`merge` is content-preserving consolidation, distinct from supersession.** The steward reaches for `merge` when the folded-away concept says something the survivor does not, so its substance is reconciled in rather than discarded. The test is **"should the survivor's content change?"** — if yes, `merge`; if no (the survivor already covers the other's ground), the steward *supersedes* instead with `C008.delete`/`deprecate` carrying a replacement, which redirects inbound links without folding any content in. Both end with the other concept gone and its links pointing at the survivor; only `merge` rewrites the survivor — which is also why the supersession path (a bare `delete`) surfaces review candidates and `merge` does not.
- **Materialization is a verb effect, not a caller step.** `create` and `revise` record recency and history (C007) and regenerate the affected index (C005) as their own terminal effects — `Creation` for `create`, `Update` for `revise` — so no caller orchestrates `record` or `reindex` and neither can be forgotten ([ADR011](../drs/ADR011-concept-verb-surface.md)). C007 owns the `timestamp` value entirely; C003 never sets it itself.
- **No OKF structural literal.** Frontmatter, slug/path, citation and link formatting, and concept-id derivation all resolve through C004, so ADR001's one-owner invariant holds with C003 present.

## Edge cases

- **`revise`/`merge` target no longer exists** (deleted or relocated since triage): C003 does not silently recreate it. It surfaces the stale direction for the steward to re-triage, mirroring C002's stale-direction handling.
- **`create` slug collides with an existing concept**: C004's slug helper disambiguates deterministically; C003 never overwrites an existing concept.
- **Admitted capture-failed task** (steward admitted despite no held material): `create` authors from the submitter's note and `origin`, cites the `origin` descriptively, and flags provenance as best-effort — there is no durable asset to promote. This is still an admitted *task*, distinct from the deferred source-less create. Consistent with C001's capture-failed record and C002's low-confidence triage of it.
- **Non-text source**: promoted into `/references/` as the version-controlled binary asset fallback and cited by path; C004 excludes declared snapshot assets from concept validation.
- **Cross-link target not yet written**: written as a forward reference; not an error (OKF §5.3). C005 resolves integrity later.
- **`revise` folds in a second source, adding beside existing claims**: `# Citations` gains an entry for the new source and individual claims carry inline markers, so a reader can trace each claim to the source it came from.
- **`revise` corrects rather than adds to a claim**: the reconciled prose reflects only the new claim, and the citation that backed the superseded claim is dropped from `# Citations` (remaining entries renumbered contiguously) — not left listed with nothing in the body pointing to it ([ADR010](../drs/ADR010-citation-pruning-on-merge.md)).
- **Steward-direction `revise` on a concept with no new source**: refines the existing body and leaves citations untouched; recorded as `Update`. If the steward's direction would introduce a claim no cited source backs, C003 declines to author it uncited (O001-R001) and surfaces that a source must be ingested first.
- **Source drifts after authoring**: not C003's concern — C006 - Source Revalidation detects drift by comparing the citation's live locator against the archived `/references/` asset, hashed on demand rather than against any stored baseline; the steward then corrects the concept via `revise`.

## Relationships

- **C002 - Triage**: `create` and `revise`-from-task consume an admitted task's `triage.decision` and `direction` (`merge` with a `target-concept-id`, or `keep-new`) plus any note, read from C001's task record. C002 decides whether and what to admit; C003 executes how. A `direction` gone stale between admission and authoring is surfaced for re-triage, not acted on blindly. A steward-direction `revise` does not pass through C002.
- **C001 - Ingestion Queue**: `create` and `revise`-from-task read `get_task` and `durable_ref`; promote the staged snapshot into `/references/`, settling the promotion boundary C001 deferred. C001 durably holds the source until promotion; C003 performs the relocation and citation. A rejected task never reaches C003 (C001 discards it at triage).
- **C004 - OKF Conformance**: the sole authoring path — serialize frontmatter, derive slug/path and concept-id, render the `# Citations` block and bundle-relative links. C003 encodes no OKF literal and may call `validate` on the authored concept. ADR001's one-owner invariant holds with C003 present.
- **C005 - Index & Navigation**: C003 authors concepts and their reasoned cross-links (O005-R002); C005 maintains the navigable index and inbound-link integrity over that output. `create`/`revise` trigger a reindex as an internal terminal effect. No hard dependency for authoring — link-candidate discovery is self-contained in C003 today (agent scan through C004, reusing C002's overlap candidates), and can later delegate to C005's index/graph without changing this contract (ADR004 precedent).
- **C007 - Currency Tracking**: `create` records `Creation` and `revise` records `Update` as their terminal effects, so authoring is the first event in a concept's history; recording recency (the `timestamp` value) and the dated change log is C007's alone — C003 emits the concept through C004 but never sets `timestamp` itself.
- **C006 - Source Revalidation**: reads its citation's live `origin` URL (captured-snapshot source) or in-place path (in-place source) to know what to revalidate, and the promoted `/references/` asset as the drift baseline it hashes on demand — both read straight off the authored concept via C004, with no dependency on C001's task record after authoring ([ADR007](../drs/ADR007-citation-link-form-for-drift-revalidation.md)). When C006 reports drift, the steward corrects the concept through `revise`.
- **C008 - Lifecycle & Retirement**: an ordinary `revise` preserves the target and marks no supersession, so it never invokes C008. Deprecation, deletion, and moving are C008's distinct flow — a plain move is C008's `move` alone, with no C003 involvement. `merge` is the one place C003 and C008 compose: C003's `revise` folds `from` into `into`, then `C008.delete(from, replacement=into)` removes `from` and redirects its inbound links ([ADR012](../drs/ADR012-repair-to-successor-link-model.md)). A genuine supersession (new content replacing an old concept's ground) still has the steward direct `create`/`revise` for the replacement and C008 to `delete` or `deprecate` the original separately. C008 may also mechanically edit a link inside a concept C003 authored, as the narrow write exception noted above — never touching C003's authored prose beyond that one link's syntax.
- **Boundary**: C003 owns concept authoring — `create`, `revise`, and the `merge` composition — source promotion and citation, and reasoned cross-linking. It does not adjudicate admission (C002), intake or durably hold material (C001), define the format (C004), maintain the index or link integrity (C005), track recency or history (C007 — though its verbs trigger the recording), revalidate sources (C006), or delete/deprecate/move concepts (C008, though `merge` delegates the `delete`). It writes concept content only for steward-admitted tasks or steward-directed revisions.

## Success criteria

- **Nothing authored without admission or direction**: no task-sourced path writes a concept for a task whose recorded `decision` is not `admit` — verifiable by calling `create` on a held or rejected task and confirming the corpus is byte-for-byte unchanged; a `revise` acts only on a concept the steward named.
- **Every concept cited**: each authored concept carries a `# Citations` entry that resolves to its originating source (a `/references/` asset or an in-place path); a concept fused from more than one source carries inline per-claim markers. Testable by authoring and confirming the citation resolves.
- **No orphaned citations**: after any `revise`, every entry in `# Citations` is referenced by at least one inline marker in the reconciled body, and every inline marker resolves to a listed entry — verifiable by revising with a correction and confirming the superseded citation no longer appears.
- **Durable source promoted and citable**: after `create` of an external source, the source is retrievable from the corpus at the cited path independent of its origin; an already-version-controlled source is cited in place with no bytes copied.
- **`revise`/`merge` preserve referential integrity**: a `revise` keeps the concept-id and history and mints no new id; a `merge` keeps `into`'s id and redirects `from`'s inbound links to it — verifiable by revising or merging and confirming the surviving id and its inbound links remain valid.
- **`merge` is a pure composition**: a `merge` produces exactly the state of `revise(into, from)` followed by `delete(from, replacement=into)` — no separate merge-only code path — verifiable by comparing a `merge` against the two calls run in sequence.
- **Effects fire with the verb**: after any `create`/`revise`, `C007.recency` returns the verb's timestamp and the affected `index.md` reflects the change, with no separate caller call — verifiable by authoring and reading recency and the index back immediately.
- **Reasoned links**: every cross-link C003 writes has an accompanying prose reason; no link is emitted without one (OKF §5.3).
- **Intent-driven, no hand-editing**: authoring or revising a concept requires only the steward's direction; C003 performs all composition, citation, linking, and metadata (O004-R001, O004-R003).
- **One-owner invariant preserved**: C003 contains no OKF structural literal — frontmatter fields, reserved filenames, link form, or citation/index structure — all resolve through C004, so ADR001's grep check stays clean with C003 present.

## Notes

- The verb surface (`create`/`revise`/`merge`, source as a parameter, materialization as a verb effect) is captured with alternatives in [ADR011](../drs/ADR011-concept-verb-surface.md); it supersedes the earlier `author(keep-new|merge)` framing while retaining ADR005's provenance, citation-granularity, and merge-reconciliation substance.
- The provenance area (`/references/` at the bundle root) and the cite-by-path model — a promoted source is an **asset, not a concept** — settle the corpus-side provenance layout ADR002 deferred to C003, and are captured with the citation-granularity and reconciliation decisions in [ADR005](../drs/ADR005-integration-authoring-provenance-and-merge.md).
- Citation pruning on `revise` — recomputing `# Citations` from the reconciled body rather than only ever unioning in — is captured with alternatives in [ADR010](../drs/ADR010-citation-pruning-on-merge.md) and [CR002](../../crs/CR002-citation-pruning-on-merge.md).
- Authoring is the terminal write of the product's **Ingest** operation, surfaced through the curation Agent Skill: the steward directs; the tool authors, cites, and links. `merge` and steward-direction `revise` are also directed from **Survey** findings (duplication and drift).
- Link-candidate discovery is a self-contained agent scan through C004, reusing C002's overlap candidates when available — no C005 dependency, consistent with [ADR004](../drs/ADR004-triage-disposition-model.md), and optimizable later by delegating to C005.
- Source-less `create` (a new concept from the steward's knowledge with no task) is deferred to a future PDR; `create` stays admitted-task-gated ([ADR011](../drs/ADR011-concept-verb-surface.md)).
