# C003 - Integration Authoring

Turns a triaged item, another concept, or the steward's direction into concept content ŌĆö composing it, attaching its source citation, and cross-linking related concepts with the reason for each stated ŌĆö through two write verbs, `create` (a new concept) and `revise` (an existing one in place), and the `merge` composition built from them.

C003 is the curation pipeline's **write stage** and the only component that *authors* concept content ŌĆö composing prose, citations, and reasoned links. Its verbs realize the concept surface's write half ([ADR011](../drs/ADR011-concept-verb-surface.md)): `create` mints a new concept from an admitted task; `revise` folds a source into an existing concept in place; `merge` combines two existing concepts as a composition of `revise` + C008's `delete`. C008 - Lifecycle & Retirement additionally performs narrow, mechanical link-repair edits to concepts it isn't itself acting on (stripping, retargeting, or redirecting a link's syntax when it deletes, moves, or merges that link's target), so C003 is not the *only* writer of any kind, just the only author (see [ADR009](../drs/ADR009-retirement-relocation-and-link-repair-model.md), [ADR012](../drs/ADR012-repair-to-successor-link-model.md)). It fulfills cited claims (O001-R001) and intent-driven authoring (O004-R001), and is the remediation half of assisted upkeep (O004-R003): `revise` from the steward's direction corrects an existing concept by intent, so the detect faces' findings are acted on with no hand-editing of the corpus. It shares reasoned cross-links (O005-R002) with C005 - Index & Navigation: C003 authors the link with its reason, C005 makes the graph navigable and keeps it intact. As the only component that authors claims, it also owns faithful representation (O009): as it composes, it checks each claim against the cited source it was drawn from and flags unsupported claims for the steward (O009-R001), presents each claim beside its source span for confirmation before the write commits (O009-R003), and persists a per-claim grounding signal any later consumer can read (O009-R002) ŌĆö the fidelity sibling of the traceability O001-R001 already gives every claim ([ADR015](../drs/ADR015-claim-grounding-seam.md)).

Every write verb is **atomic including its effects**: composing content, promoting provenance, recomputing citations, recording recency and history (C007), and regenerating the affected index (C005) all happen as part of the verb, not as separate calls a caller must remember to make afterward ([ADR011](../drs/ADR011-concept-verb-surface.md)). One capability is deferred: authoring a *new* concept from the steward's own knowledge with no task (source-less `create`) awaits a future PDR, so `create` stays admitted-task-gated; revising an existing, already-provenanced concept from steward direction is in scope.

## Data model

C003 owns **no staging artifact of its own**. Its output is the corpus itself ŌĆö concepts and the provenance assets they cite ŌĆö whose format is C004's, not C003's. C003 owns the *composition*, and it settles one path convention ADR002 deferred to it.

- **Authored concept.** An OKF concept C003 composes and writes via C004: frontmatter (a non-empty `type`, a `title`, and any steward-supplied metadata; the `timestamp` value is C007's, set as a verb effect), a structural markdown body, a `# Citations` block, and cross-links. C003 owns which fields and content go in; C004 owns the surrounding format.
- **Provenance area** (`/references/`, bundle root). The corpus-side location a durable source is promoted into at authoring ŌĆö settling the promotion boundary C001 and [ADR002](../drs/ADR002-intake-staging-and-durable-source.md) left open. A promoted source is a **git-tracked asset, not a concept**: UTF-8 text for textual sources, or the bounded binary-asset fallback for non-text sources. C004's plain-text-concept `error` rule excludes declared snapshot assets, so a promoted binary source never reads as a conformance failure.
- **Citation.** A numbered `# Citations` block (OKF ┬¦8), rendered through C004's citation helper. **Concept-level by default**: a single-source concept's block covers all its claims. **Inline per-claim** only when a concept draws on more than one source (after a `revise` that folds in a second source, or a `merge`): individual claims carry numbered markers so a reader can tell which source backs which claim. **Link target depends on how the source was durably held** ([ADR007](../drs/ADR007-citation-link-form-for-drift-revalidation.md)): an in-place source cites its existing repo path, unchanged; a captured-snapshot source cites the live `origin` URL as the primary link (OKF ┬¦8 permits an absolute-URL citation), naming the archived `/references/` asset as a durable fallback copy in the surrounding prose. This keeps the citation pointed at the actual external material and gives C006 - Source Revalidation a live locator to read directly off the concept. **The block always mirrors the body it accompanies**: on a `revise`, C003 recomputes it from the reconciled body's inline markers ŌĆö a citation no claim still references is dropped, and the rest renumbered contiguously ([ADR010](../drs/ADR010-citation-pruning-on-merge.md)) ŌĆö the same way a fresh `create` derives its citation block from what's actually written rather than carrying anything forward unreferenced.
- **Cross-link.** A bundle-relative link (`/ŌĆ”`, C004's link helper) whose **reason is stated in the surrounding prose**, because OKF ┬¦5.3 conveys the relationship kind in prose, not in the link itself. C003 emits no link without its reason.
- **Grounding signal.** A per-claim marker recording how well each authored claim is grounded in its cited source ŌĆö the persisted form of `check_grounding`'s verdict (`supported` / `unsupported` / `partial` / `uncheckable`). Its **format is a C004-owned Wiki authoring convention** (a per-claim body marker under OKF's producer-extension allowance, OKF ┬¦4.2/┬¦9, independent of the citation-numbering markers), so no OKF change is needed; its **value is C003's**, computed at authoring and materialized as a terminal verb effect of `create`/`revise` so it always mirrors the claims it accompanies. This is the same shape-vs-value split C004 and C007 hold for `timestamp`. Consumers read the signal directly off the concept via Query (O009-R002).

## Interfaces

C003 exposes an in-process face. Every read of a task goes through C001, every OKF read/write goes through C004, and the only corpus writes are concept content and its promoted provenance asset.

- **`create(task_id) ŌåÆ concept_id`** ŌĆö mints a **new** concept. Refuses unless the task's recorded `decision` is `admit`. Reads the task and its `durable_ref` (C001); promotes the durable source into `/references/`; composes the concept and its `# Citations` via C004; writes confirmed cross-links; writes the concept file at a C004-derived slug/path; and, as terminal effects, records `Creation` (C007) and reindexes (C005). Returns the concept's C004-derived id. (This is the former `author` with `direction.mode = keep-new`.)
- **`revise(concept_id, source) ŌåÆ concept_id`** ŌĆö folds `source` into an **existing** concept **in place**, preserving its id, history, and inbound links, and recomputing citations to match the reconciled body. `source` is one of: an **admitted task** (the former `author` with `direction.mode = merge` ŌĆö unions cross-links, reconciles prose per the steward's note, promotes and cites the task's source); **another concept** (the reconciliation step `merge` builds on); or the **steward's direction** (a correction or refinement to an existing, already-provenanced concept ŌĆö O004-R003, the interface C006 and C010 reference for correcting content). Records `Update` (C007) and reindexes (C005) as terminal effects.
- **`merge(from_concept_id, into_concept_id, [note]) ŌåÆ concept_id`** ŌĆö combines two **existing** concepts, defined as `revise(into_concept_id, source=from_concept_id)` followed by `C008.delete(from_concept_id, replacement=into_concept_id, content_preserved=true)`. The surviving `into` concept keeps its id and history; `from`'s inbound links are redirected to `into` by C008's repair-to-successor rule ([ADR012](../drs/ADR012-repair-to-successor-link-model.md)), and `merge` surfaces no review candidates because it passes `content_preserved=true` ŌĆö its own `revise` step already folded the content into `into` ([ADR014](../drs/ADR014-merge-composition-integrity.md)). The two steps run in **one transaction**: `merge` commits as a single unit or not at all (one git commit or none), so a failure of either step leaves the corpus byte-for-byte unchanged ŌĆö no fold without removal, no removal without fold. Returns the surviving `into_concept_id`. Fulfills the in-corpus case of the duplication-remediation choice O003-R003 already names.
- **`promote_source(task) ŌåÆ citation_ref`** *(internal effect of `create`/`revise`-from-task)* ŌĆö promotes the durable source from C001's staging into `/references/` and returns the citable bundle-relative path; for an already-version-controlled in-place source, returns the existing repo path and copies no bytes. Not a steward-facing operation.
- **`propose_links(concept | task_id) ŌåÆ link_candidate[]`** *(internal, advisory, read-only)* ŌĆö the cross-link candidate scan: reuses C002's overlap candidates when present, else runs its own agent scan reading corpus concepts through C004, returning each candidate with a suggested reason for the steward to confirm, drop, or edit. It writes nothing; `create`/`revise` only write links the steward confirmed. Self-contained: no C005 dependency ([ADR004](../drs/ADR004-triage-disposition-model.md) precedent).
- **`check_grounding(concept | draft) ŌåÆ claim_grounding[]`** *(internal, advisory, read-only)* ŌĆö the source-grounding pass: for each authored claim, pairs it with the span of the cited source it was drawn from and returns a support verdict (`supported` / `unsupported` / `partial` / `uncheckable`) with a rationale, reading the draft and its promoted source through C004. Self-contained like `propose_links` ŌĆö a heuristic agent judgment about representation, the semantic sibling of C006's mechanical byte-level drift comparison, not a mechanical check. It writes nothing; `create`/`revise` present its output as the distillation review the steward confirms (O009-R003), surface its non-`supported` claims for judgment (O009-R001), and persist its verdict as the per-claim grounding signal (O009-R002). May later delegate to a shared grounding owner or be reused by a post-drift re-check without changing this contract ([ADR015](../drs/ADR015-claim-grounding-seam.md); see Notes).

## Behavior

- **`create`/`revise`-from-task author only what the steward admitted.** No task-sourced path writes a concept for a task whose recorded `decision` is not `admit`. Integration stays never-automatic: C002 decides *whether and what*, C003 does *how*, one admitted item at a time. A steward-direction `revise` acts on an existing concept the steward names directly ŌĆö its adjudication is the steward's own direction, not a triage gate.
- **Intent in, authoring out.** From the admitted task, another concept, or the steward's direction and note, C003 performs all composition, citation, linking, and metadata. The steward never hand-edits the corpus (O004-R001, O004-R003).
- **Cite what it writes ŌĆö and only what it still writes.** Every authored concept carries a `# Citations` entry resolving to its originating source (O001-R001) ŌĆö a promoted `/references/` asset, or the in-place repo path for an already-versioned source. A single source covers the whole concept; a concept fused from more than one source adds inline per-claim markers so provenance stays legible. When a `revise` *corrects* a claim rather than adding beside it, the superseded citation is dropped along with the claim it backed, not carried forward unreferenced ([ADR010](../drs/ADR010-citation-pruning-on-merge.md)) ŌĆö the correction and its prior citation remain fully recoverable via git history and C007's `history`, just no longer live in the document. A steward-direction `revise` that only refines wording or structure changes no citation; it must not introduce a net-new claim without a source (that comes through a task).
- **Check grounding, flag distortion for the steward.** As it composes, C003 runs `check_grounding` over the draft's claims against their cited source and presents each claim beside the source span it was drawn from ŌĆö the distillation review ŌĆö before the write commits (O009-R003). Claims the source does not support (`unsupported` / `partial`) are surfaced for the steward's judgment (O009-R001), never silently admitted; this extends C003's refusal to author an *uncited* claim to the *unfaithfully-cited* case ŌĆö a source present but not backing the claim. The check recommends, it never decides: fidelity is heuristic, so the steward confirms, edits, or overrides, and only a confirmed draft is written (intent in, work out).
- **Promote at authoring, cite by locator.** The durable source C001 held in staging is promoted into `/references/` as a git-tracked asset regardless of citation form ŌĆö the archived copy always exists as the drift baseline C006 later hashes on demand. The citation link itself follows the source's kind ([ADR007](../drs/ADR007-citation-link-form-for-drift-revalidation.md)): an in-place source already version-controlled alongside the corpus is cited at its existing path with no copy; a captured-snapshot source is cited by its live `origin` URL, with the archived `/references/` path named alongside it in prose. Because nothing outside the task pointed at the staging path, the one-time relocation of the archived copy is safe (ADR002).
- **Reasoned cross-links.** Links are authored with their reason in prose (OKF ┬¦5.3). Candidates are proposed by `propose_links`; the steward confirms; the write verb writes only confirmed links. A link target that is not yet written is allowed ŌĆö OKF ┬¦5.3 requires consumers to tolerate not-yet-written knowledge, and inbound-link integrity is C005's, not a block on authoring.
- **`revise` preserves identity.** A `revise` folds its source into the existing concept **in place**: it keeps the concept-id and history and recomputes citations to match the reconciled body rather than blindly unioning the old set with the new ŌĆö a claim that survives reconciliation keeps its citation; a claim the revision corrects away takes its citation with it ([ADR010](../drs/ADR010-citation-pruning-on-merge.md)). No new id is minted, so inbound links stay valid ŌĆö referential integrity holds by construction. `create` instead mints a fresh concept.
- **`merge` composes, never re-implements.** `merge` is exactly `revise` (fold `from` into `into`) plus `C008.delete(from, replacement=into, content_preserved=true)`; it introduces no third authoring or removal mechanism. The one difference from a standalone supersession `delete` is the `content_preserved=true` argument ŌĆö a truthful assertion the `revise` step made valid, not a merge-only code path ([ADR014](../drs/ADR014-merge-composition-integrity.md)). The redirect of `from`'s inbound links and the `Retirement` log entry for `from` are C008's, via ADR012's repair-to-successor rule.
- **`merge` is atomic as a composition.** Per-verb atomicity ([ADR011](../drs/ADR011-concept-verb-surface.md)) does not by itself make a two-verb composition atomic, so `merge` carries its own contract: its `revise` and `delete` steps stage into one transaction and materialize as a single unit or not at all ŌĆö one git commit or none. A `delete` failure after `revise` has folded content into `into` cannot leave the content in `into` *and* the original `from` present; the whole `merge` rolls back, so the tool never manufactures the duplicate (O003-RSK003) `merge` exists to resolve ([ADR014](../drs/ADR014-merge-composition-integrity.md)).
- **`merge` is content-preserving consolidation, distinct from supersession.** The steward reaches for `merge` when the folded-away concept says something the survivor does not, so its substance is reconciled in rather than discarded. The test is **"should the survivor's content change?"** ŌĆö if yes, `merge`; if no (the survivor already covers the other's ground), the steward *supersedes* instead with `C008.delete`/`deprecate` carrying a replacement, which redirects inbound links without folding any content in. Both end with the other concept gone and its links pointing at the survivor; only `merge` rewrites the survivor ŌĆö which is also why the supersession path (a bare `delete`) surfaces review candidates and `merge` does not.
- **Materialization is a verb effect, not a caller step.** `create` and `revise` record recency and history (C007), regenerate the affected index (C005), and write the per-claim grounding signal (O009-R002) as their own terminal effects ŌĆö `Creation` for `create`, `Update` for `revise` ŌĆö so no caller orchestrates `record`, `reindex`, or the grounding signal and none can be forgotten ([ADR011](../drs/ADR011-concept-verb-surface.md), [ADR015](../drs/ADR015-claim-grounding-seam.md)). The signal is written in the same terminal step as the claims it describes, so a claim's legible grounding never drifts from the claim itself. C007 owns the `timestamp` value entirely; C003 never sets it itself.
- **No OKF structural literal.** Frontmatter, slug/path, citation and link formatting, and concept-id derivation all resolve through C004, so ADR001's one-owner invariant holds with C003 present.

## Edge cases

- **`revise`/`merge` target no longer exists** (deleted or relocated since triage): C003 does not silently recreate it. It surfaces the stale direction for the steward to re-triage, mirroring C002's stale-direction handling.
- **`create` slug collides with an existing concept**: C004's slug helper disambiguates deterministically; C003 never overwrites an existing concept.
- **Admitted capture-failed task** (steward admitted despite no held material): `create` authors from the submitter's note and `origin`, cites the `origin` descriptively, and flags provenance as best-effort ŌĆö there is no durable asset to promote. This is still an admitted *task*, distinct from the deferred source-less create. Consistent with C001's capture-failed record and C002's low-confidence triage of it.
- **Non-text source**: promoted into `/references/` as the version-controlled binary asset fallback and cited by path; C004 excludes declared snapshot assets from concept validation.
- **Cross-link target not yet written**: written as a forward reference; not an error (OKF ┬¦5.3). C005 resolves integrity later.
- **`revise` folds in a second source, adding beside existing claims**: `# Citations` gains an entry for the new source and individual claims carry inline markers, so a reader can trace each claim to the source it came from.
- **`revise` corrects rather than adds to a claim**: the reconciled prose reflects only the new claim, and the citation that backed the superseded claim is dropped from `# Citations` (remaining entries renumbered contiguously) ŌĆö not left listed with nothing in the body pointing to it ([ADR010](../drs/ADR010-citation-pruning-on-merge.md)).
- **Steward-direction `revise` on a concept with no new source**: refines the existing body and leaves citations untouched; recorded as `Update`. If the steward's direction would introduce a claim no cited source backs, C003 declines to author it uncited (O001-R001) and surfaces that a source must be ingested first.
- **Authored claim its cited source does not support** (`check_grounding` returns `unsupported` or `partial`): surfaced in the distillation review for the steward's judgment, never written silently (O009-R001). The steward tightens the claim to what the source backs, overrides (the recorded signal then reflects the override), or ingests a better source ŌĆö C003 does not resolve the distortion itself. Distinct from the uncited case above: here a source is present but does not back the claim.
- **Grounding uncheckable** (a best-effort or descriptive-only citation from a capture-failed admitted task, or a source span C003 cannot resolve to compare against): `check_grounding` returns `uncheckable` and the grounding signal records that, never `supported` ŌĆö the fidelity analogue of C006's `not-revalidatable`-vs-`unchanged` distinction, so an unverifiable claim is not misread as a source-backed one.
- **Source drifts after authoring**: not C003's concern ŌĆö C006 - Source Revalidation detects drift by comparing the citation's live locator against the archived `/references/` asset, hashed on demand rather than against any stored baseline; the steward then corrects the concept via `revise`.

## Relationships

- **C002 - Triage**: `create` and `revise`-from-task consume an admitted task's `triage.decision` and `direction` (`merge` with a `target-concept-id`, or `keep-new`) plus any note, read from C001's task record. C002 decides whether and what to admit; C003 executes how. A `direction` gone stale between admission and authoring is surfaced for re-triage, not acted on blindly. A steward-direction `revise` does not pass through C002.
- **C001 - Ingestion Queue**: `create` and `revise`-from-task read `get_task` and `durable_ref`; promote the staged snapshot into `/references/`, settling the promotion boundary C001 deferred. C001 durably holds the source until promotion; C003 performs the relocation and citation. A rejected task never reaches C003 (C001 discards it at triage).
- **C004 - OKF Conformance**: the sole authoring path ŌĆö serialize frontmatter, derive slug/path and concept-id, render the `# Citations` block, bundle-relative links, and the per-claim grounding-signal marker (a Wiki authoring convention C004 owns, under OKF's producer-extension allowance). C003 supplies each marker's value ŌĆö the `check_grounding` verdict ŌĆö but encodes no OKF literal for its shape, and may call `validate` on the authored concept. ADR001's one-owner invariant holds with C003 present.
- **C005 - Index & Navigation**: C003 authors concepts and their reasoned cross-links (O005-R002); C005 maintains the navigable index and inbound-link integrity over that output. `create`/`revise` trigger a reindex as an internal terminal effect. No hard dependency for authoring ŌĆö link-candidate discovery is self-contained in C003 today (agent scan through C004, reusing C002's overlap candidates), and can later delegate to C005's index/graph without changing this contract (ADR004 precedent).
- **C007 - Currency Tracking**: `create` records `Creation` and `revise` records `Update` as their terminal effects, so authoring is the first event in a concept's history; recording recency (the `timestamp` value) and the dated change log is C007's alone ŌĆö C003 emits the concept through C004 but never sets `timestamp` itself.
- **C006 - Source Revalidation**: reads its citation's live `origin` URL (captured-snapshot source) or in-place path (in-place source) to know what to revalidate, and the promoted `/references/` asset as the drift baseline it hashes on demand ŌĆö both read straight off the authored concept via C004, with no dependency on C001's task record after authoring ([ADR007](../drs/ADR007-citation-link-form-for-drift-revalidation.md)). When C006 reports drift, the steward corrects the concept through `revise` ŌĆö which re-runs `check_grounding`, so the grounding signal is refreshed against the corrected claim. C006 and C003's grounding check are complementary, not overlapping: C006 asks *has the source moved?* (mechanical, post-authoring), `check_grounding` asks *does the claim match the source?* (semantic, at authoring); a future post-drift re-check could reuse `check_grounding` behind the delegation seam (see Notes).
- **C008 - Lifecycle & Retirement**: an ordinary `revise` preserves the target and marks no supersession, so it never invokes C008. Deprecation, deletion, and moving are C008's distinct flow ŌĆö a plain move is C008's `move` alone, with no C003 involvement. `merge` is the one place C003 and C008 compose: C003's `revise` folds `from` into `into`, then `C008.delete(from, replacement=into, content_preserved=true)` removes `from` and redirects its inbound links ([ADR012](../drs/ADR012-repair-to-successor-link-model.md)), the two steps committing as one transaction ([ADR014](../drs/ADR014-merge-composition-integrity.md)). A genuine supersession (new content replacing an old concept's ground) still has the steward direct `create`/`revise` for the replacement and C008 to `delete` or `deprecate` the original separately. C008 may also mechanically edit a link inside a concept C003 authored, as the narrow write exception noted above ŌĆö never touching C003's authored prose beyond that one link's syntax.
- **Boundary**: C003 owns concept authoring ŌĆö `create`, `revise`, and the `merge` composition ŌĆö source promotion and citation, reasoned cross-linking, and the author-time grounding check that keeps each claim faithful to its source (O009). It does not adjudicate admission (C002), intake or durably hold material (C001), define the format (C004 ŌĆö including the grounding-signal marker's shape), maintain the index or link integrity (C005), track recency or history (C007 ŌĆö though its verbs trigger the recording), revalidate sources for drift (C006), or delete/deprecate/move concepts (C008, though `merge` delegates the `delete`). It writes concept content only for steward-admitted tasks or steward-directed revisions.

## Success criteria

- **Nothing authored without admission or direction**: no task-sourced path writes a concept for a task whose recorded `decision` is not `admit` ŌĆö verifiable by calling `create` on a held or rejected task and confirming the corpus is byte-for-byte unchanged; a `revise` acts only on a concept the steward named.
- **Every concept cited**: each authored concept carries a `# Citations` entry that resolves to its originating source (a `/references/` asset or an in-place path); a concept fused from more than one source carries inline per-claim markers. Testable by authoring and confirming the citation resolves.
- **No orphaned citations**: after any `revise`, every entry in `# Citations` is referenced by at least one inline marker in the reconciled body, and every inline marker resolves to a listed entry ŌĆö verifiable by revising with a correction and confirming the superseded citation no longer appears.
- **Durable source promoted and citable**: after `create` of an external source, the source is retrievable from the corpus at the cited path independent of its origin; an already-version-controlled source is cited in place with no bytes copied.
- **`revise`/`merge` preserve referential integrity**: a `revise` keeps the concept-id and history and mints no new id; a `merge` keeps `into`'s id and redirects `from`'s inbound links to it ŌĆö verifiable by revising or merging and confirming the surviving id and its inbound links remain valid.
- **`merge` is a pure composition**: a `merge` produces the same corpus bytes and the same repaired links as `revise(into, from)` followed by `delete(from, replacement=into, content_preserved=true)`, and ŌĆö passing that same flag ŌĆö the same (empty) review report; there is no separate merge-only code path. Verifiable by comparing a `merge` against those two calls run in sequence with `content_preserved=true`; the only thing that distinguishes `merge`'s `delete` from a supersession `delete` is that argument value.
- **`merge` is atomic**: a `merge` commits as one unit or not at all (one git commit or none) ŌĆö verifiable by forcing its `delete` step to fail and confirming the corpus is byte-for-byte unchanged (the `revise` fold is not left behind and `from` is not removed).
- **Effects fire with the verb**: after any `create`/`revise`, `C007.recency` returns the verb's timestamp and the affected `index.md` reflects the change, with no separate caller call ŌĆö verifiable by authoring and reading recency and the index back immediately.
- **Reasoned links**: every cross-link C003 writes has an accompanying prose reason; no link is emitted without one (OKF ┬¦5.3).
- **Unsupported claims flagged, never silently written**: a draft claim its cited source does not support surfaces in the distillation review with a non-`supported` verdict and is not written until the steward confirms, edits, or overrides ŌĆö verifiable by authoring a claim the source contradicts and confirming it does not enter the corpus unflagged (O009-R001, O009-R003).
- **Grounding signal materialized per claim**: after any `create`/`revise`, each authored claim carries a grounding signal reflecting `check_grounding`'s verdict, readable directly off the concept, with no separate caller call ŌĆö verifiable by authoring and reading the signal back; an uncheckable claim reads `uncheckable`, never `supported` (O009-R002).
- **Intent-driven, no hand-editing**: authoring or revising a concept requires only the steward's direction; C003 performs all composition, citation, linking, and metadata (O004-R001, O004-R003).
- **One-owner invariant preserved**: C003 contains no OKF structural literal ŌĆö frontmatter fields, reserved filenames, link form, or citation/index structure ŌĆö all resolve through C004, so ADR001's grep check stays clean with C003 present.

## Notes

- The verb surface (`create`/`revise`/`merge`, source as a parameter, materialization as a verb effect) is captured with alternatives in [ADR011](../drs/ADR011-concept-verb-surface.md); it supersedes the earlier `author(keep-new|merge)` framing while retaining ADR005's provenance, citation-granularity, and merge-reconciliation substance.
- The provenance area (`/references/` at the bundle root) and the cite-by-path model ŌĆö a promoted source is an **asset, not a concept** ŌĆö settle the corpus-side provenance layout ADR002 deferred to C003, and are captured with the citation-granularity and reconciliation decisions in [ADR005](../drs/ADR005-integration-authoring-provenance-and-merge.md).
- Citation pruning on `revise` ŌĆö recomputing `# Citations` from the reconciled body rather than only ever unioning in ŌĆö is captured with alternatives in [ADR010](../drs/ADR010-citation-pruning-on-merge.md) and [CR002](../../crs/CR002-citation-pruning-on-merge.md).
- What keeps `merge` a genuine composition ŌĆö the `content_preserved=true` argument its `delete` step passes (so review-candidate suppression is a truthful flag, not a merge-only branch) and the one-commit-or-none atomicity of the two-step composition ŌĆö is captured with alternatives in [ADR014](../drs/ADR014-merge-composition-integrity.md) and [CR005](../../crs/CR005-merge-composition-integrity.md).
- Authoring is the terminal write of the product's **Ingest** operation, surfaced through the curation Agent Skill: the steward directs; the tool authors, cites, and links. `merge` and steward-direction `revise` are also directed from **Survey** findings (duplication and drift).
- Link-candidate discovery is a self-contained agent scan through C004, reusing C002's overlap candidates when available ŌĆö no C005 dependency, consistent with [ADR004](../drs/ADR004-triage-disposition-model.md), and optimizable later by delegating to C005.
- Source-less `create` (a new concept from the steward's knowledge with no task) is deferred to a future PDR; `create` stays admitted-task-gated ([ADR011](../drs/ADR011-concept-verb-surface.md)).
- The author-time grounding capability (`check_grounding`, the distillation review, and the per-claim grounding signal) owns O009-R001/R002/R003; the seam placement ŌĆö on C003 rather than a dedicated fidelity component ŌĆö is captured with alternatives in [ADR015](../drs/ADR015-claim-grounding-seam.md) and [CR007](../../crs/CR007-claim-grounding-trace.md). `check_grounding` is C003-internal and self-contained today (one caller), like `propose_links`; a **delegation seam** is reserved so that if a second caller appears ŌĆö a shared corpus-grounding owner (cf. the five-scan consolidation the architecture flags) or a C006 post-drift re-check ŌĆö the judgment can be lifted behind a shared interface without changing C003's contract, the same forward path `propose_links`/`assess_overlap` hold toward C005.
